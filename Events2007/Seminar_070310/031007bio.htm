Professor Wu’s presentation

 

Information Technologies:  A Powerful Tool Enabling Value Creation

 

     The pace of information technology (IT) evolution has been both dramatic and disconcerting.  The co-evolution of technology, work, and skills of the workforce over the past 30 years has dramatically changed our concept of how business organizations compete.  No long serving simply as a passive tool in the “back-office”, IT has become a strategic part of most businesses, enabling creation of new products and services and consequently redefinition of the market and industries.

 

     In this presentation, I will first define the frequently-mentioned business slogan “value creation.”  I will relate the definition to the concept of “value chain” then proceed to explain how business, with different business strategies, creates value for stakeholders, including customers, stockholders, suppliers, employees and society as the whole.

 

     The thrust of my presentation is to use examples (real firms) to illustrate how business organizations deploy IT to complement business strategies and create value.

 

 

Professor Wu’s Biography:

 

Frederick H. Wu, Ph.D., CMA received his Ph.D. from Texas Tech University with Concentrations in accounting, statistics, and computer science.  Professor Wu began his teaching career at Wichita State University in 1974 and became chairman/director of the School of Accountancy at Southern Illinois University at Carbondale.  In 1993 he moved to the University of North Texas and served as chair of the Department of Accounting.  He went back to full-time teaching in 1997 until 2006 when he retired.  He served twice, under the financial support of the National Science Academy of Taiwan, to teach at National Cheng Kung University and was nominated, as a candidate, to be the president of the university. 

 

Professor Wu’s teaching and research interests are in the areas of information systems and management accounting.  The courses he taught in the past include Accounting Information Systems, Decision Support/Expert Systems, Management Control Systems and Information Technology, Strategic Information Systems, and Cast/managerial Accounting.  As the mentor, he chaired several doctoral dissertations of international students from Egypt, Japan and Taiwan.

 

He has published three textbooks in accounting information systems and 30 plus articles in various academic and professional journals.  He was awarded with Certificates of Merit for outstanding articles published in Management Accounting in 1974 and 1975.  He also presented papers at numerous conferences in Taiwan, Hong Kong, China, Japan, Mexico, France, Canada, and Egypt.  He served on the editorial boards of the Journal of Information Systems, the Journal of Accounting Education of the AAA, the International Journal of Applied Quality Management, and Applications of fuzzy Sets and the Theory of Evidence to Accounting, Information Systems and Change management, Journal of Accounting and Corporate Governance.

 

Professor Wu is a member of the American Accounting Association, Institute of Management Accounting, Financial Executives Institute, and Information Systems Audit and Control Association.  Professor Wu’s business experience includes working as accounting manager and controller of Pfizer International and systems auditor of Fox Company.  E-Mail: wu@unt.edu

C. Y. Chang (張謙益)

 

國防科技與民間產業衍生應用的探討

 

張謙益, 國立台灣大學電機系學士, 美國 Rice University 電機系博士, 主修資訊科技, 圖式辨認, 及形象、信號處理. 曾兼任授課於 University of Houston at Clear Lake 和 University of Texas at Dallas.  現已退休, 係為 a Life Senior Member of IEEE.  他於 2004 年底協同籌劃創立北美台灣工程師協會達拉斯分會, 並任首屆會長.  

在退休前, 張謙益大部分在美國國防工業有關的公司服務. 早期也曾在美國太空中心 NASA 以及石油界相關公司服務過. 他去年六月獲得國科會錄取參與伯樂計劃, 得有機會返台回饋台灣為鄉土服務. 在短短一個多月期間, 加入國家實驗研究院技策資訊中心一研究小組, 參與國防科技與民間產業衍生應用的探討. 他會將此參與伯樂計劃服務行程及心得感想於三月十日和 NATEA-Dallas 會員分享.

 

C.Y. Chang received his B.S. from National Taiwan University and Ph.D from Rice University, all in electrical engineering where he majored in information theory and image & signal processing areas. He had been part-time faculty at the University of Houston at Clear Lake as well as the University of Texas at Dallas for a brief period of time. He is currently a Life Senior Member in IEEE. He was instrumental in founding the Dallas Chapter of North America Taiwanese Engineers’ association (NATEA-Dallas) in December, 2004 where he served as the Chapter’s first president. Presently he is a member of Board of Directors both at NATEA Headquarters as well as NATEA-Dallas.

 

For the past 35 years until retirement, C.Y. had been working with Fortune 500 companies – mostly in the US defense industry with the exception of a brief period in the oil industry. During those years, he was primarily involved in the algorithms development and program management in the areas of statistical pattern recognition, automatic target detection, computer vision, and image & signal processing for military applications, and in the areas of multispectral pattern recognition and digital signal processing and adaptive filtering applicable to NASA’s remote sensing as well as exploration seismic data processing programs. His professional careers were culminated as a recipient of Raytheon Corporation’s Excellence in Technology Award in 2002.

 

C.Y. was among the first batch of Elite Project Groups, sponsored by Taiwan’s National Science Council (NSC), designed for retirees and/or near-retire high-tech professionals from Overseas Taiwanese, to provide short-term consultation services to Taiwan’s government or private institutions in the areas of respective expertise. He participated in the said Elite Project from June to July of 2006. His primary consultation services were focused on the government’s policy studies in terms of a critical look at both military and commercial high-tech areas where relevant defense industry technologies could be mutually leveraged upon. He will share such experiences along with promoting recruitment efforts from Taiwan NSC’s Elite Project with NATEA-Dallas members in the forthcoming March 10th seminar.
